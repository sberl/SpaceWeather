Deep Space Network

A Coronal Mass Ejection plasma cloud causes geomagnetic storms on Earth and may cause power grid failures that could affect the Deep Space Network antennas - temporarily suspending communications.

The NASA Deep Space Network is an international network of antennas that supports interplanetary spacecraft missions.

Continuous 24-hour coverage requires several stations at locations that compensate for Earth's daily rotation.

The locations in Spain, Australia, and California are approximately 120 degrees apart in longitude which enables continuous observation and overlap for transferring the spacecraft radio link from one complex to the next.
