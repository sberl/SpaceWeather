Cosmic Radiation

Definition:
High energy particles originating from outer space and our Sun. When compared to X-rays or Gamma rays – cosmic radiation cause more biological damage.

More Information:

During our mission to Mars, we will be leaving the protection of Earth's magnetosphere. Earth's magnetic field shields humans from most cosmic rays, but they are common in interplanetary space.

Cosmic rays penetrate most forms of shielding, and so remain a hazard to human health. Crew who are in space for long periods of time get an increasing dosage of these rays.

Each member of the Mars Lander crew has a radiation badge. The radiation level must be monitored periodically.

One of two readings will occur:

No radiation exposure – the radiation sensor will stay green in color, or

High level of exposure – the radiation sensor will show bright red. This is an emergency. Contact the MISSION COMMANDER immediately.
