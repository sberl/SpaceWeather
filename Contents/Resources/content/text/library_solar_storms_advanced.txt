<div id="research">
		
	<div id="term">
		<h2>Solar Storms</h2>
	</div>
	
	<div id="term-content">
	
		<div id="full-pane">
		
			<img class="right" src="../images/library_solar_storms.jpg" alt="Solar Storms" />
			<span id="definition">
				<p>
					<strong>Definition:</strong><br />
					A disturbance on the surface of the Sun, such as a solar flare or a coronal mass ejection (CME).
				</p>
			</span>
			<span id="more-information">
				<p>
					<strong>More Information:</strong><br />
					Tremendous heat and powerful magnetic fields cause the <strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSunhtm')">Sun</a></strong>'s 
					surface to churn and bubble. Areas of concentrated magnetism create <strong>
					<a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSunSpotshtm')">sunspots.</a></strong>
				</p>
				<p>
					The areas around  sunspots can produce intense solar storms called <strong>
					<a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSolarFlareshtm')">solar flares</a></strong> and <strong>
					<a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchCoronalMassEjectionhtm')">coronal mass ejections (CMEs)</a></strong>.
				</p>
				<p>
					A solar flare occurs  when magnetic energy builds to a peak near the Sun's surface and explodes. 
					This sends high-energy particles into space.
				</p>
				<p>
					CMEs last longer and carry a larger cloud of particles and magnetic field into space than do solar  flares.
				</p>
			</span>
