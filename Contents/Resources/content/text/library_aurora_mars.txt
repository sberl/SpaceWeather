			<span id="more-information">
				
				<p>
					<strong><a href="event:loadCItem('ResearchSolarFlareshtm','Library')">Solar flares</a></strong> can affect <strong><a href="event:loadCItem('ResearchMarshtm','Library')">Mars'</a></strong> small local <strong><a href="event:loadCItem('ResearchMagneticFieldshtm','Library')">magnetic fields</a></strong> and produce aurora. On Mars, aurora may occur in a few spots on Mars where the magnetic fields are known to be strong.
				</p>
				
				<p>
					Aurora on Mars can be observed by instruments on satellites as brief flashes of ultraviolet light (invisible to the human eye).
				</p>
				
				<p>
					The Martian aurora is composed of <strong><a href="event:loadCItem('ResearchUltravioletLighthtm','Library')">ultraviolet</a></strong> lightradiated by ions and atoms in the Martian atmosphere, the result of energetic electrically charged particles from the solar flare interacting with the Martian magnetic fields.
				</p>
