	<div id="term">
		<h2>Coronal Mass Ejection Impacts</h2>
	</div>
	<div id="term-content">
	
		<div id="full-pane">
		
			<span class="right caption long">
				<img src="../images/library_coronal_mass_ejection_impacts.jpg" alt="Coronal Mass Ejection Impacts" />
				<small class="center">A drawing of Earth's magnetosphere showing the effects of the solar wind.</small>
			</span>
		
			<span id="more-information">
				<p>
					A <strong><a href="loadToolCIndex('Research','ResearchCoronalMassEjectionhtm','Library')">coronal mass ejection (CME)</a></strong> is a cloud of electrically charged particles ejected from the Sun. 
				</p>
				<p>
					If a CME is headed in the direction of Earth or a spacecraft, it can cause the buildup of electricity in metal structures on a spacecraft and can also damage sensitive electronics.
				</p>
				<p>
					A CME interacts with Earth's magnetic field creating a <strong><a href="loadToolCIndex('Research','ResearchGeomagneticStormhtm','Library')">geomagnetic storm </a></strong> and can produce visible <strong><a href="loadToolCIndex('Research','ResearchAurorahtm','Library')">aurora</a></strong>.   
				</p>
				<p>
					On <strong><a href="loadToolCIndex('Research','ResearchMarshtm','Library')">Mars</a></strong>, a CME can produce aurora associated with very weak and scattered magnetic fields. They are invisible to the human eye but can be seen with <strong><a href="loadToolCIndex('Research','ResearchUltraviolethtm','Library')">ultraviolet</a></strong> detectors.
				</p>
				<p class="center">
					<strong><em>Mars</em></strong> does not have a strong magnetosphere like Earth. A <strong><em>CME</em></strong> that is headed towards <strong><em>Mars</em></strong> can affect equipment and Mars crews need to remain inside shielded areas. 
				</p>
			</span>
