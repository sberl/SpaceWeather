	<div id="term">
		<h2>Sun-Earth Connection</h2>
	</div>
	
	<div id="term-content">
		
		<div id="full-pane">
		
			<span id="more-information">
				
				<p>
					150 million  kilometers (93 million miles) from Earth is the star we call the <strong>
					<a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSunhtm')">Sun</a></strong>.  
					Solar energy is responsible for life on Earth and conditions on Earth including  climate, seasons, and weather.
				</p>
				<p>
					The <strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchEarthAtmospherehtm')">Earth's atmosphere</a></strong> 
					provides a protective layer that can absorb harmful  ('light') rays such as 
					<strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ReserachUltravioletLightRayshtm')">ultraviolet light</a></strong> rays. 
				</p>
				<img class="left" src="../images/library_sun_earth_connection.jpg" alt="Sun Earth Connection" />
				<p>
					The Earth's <strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchMagnetospherehtm')">magnetosphere </a></strong>
					(magnetic field) provides us with  protection from the particles that make up the 
					<strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSolarWindhtm')">solar wind.</a></strong>
				</p>
				<p>
					The solar wind is a  constant stream of electrified particles coming from the 
					<a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSunhtm')"><strong>Sun</strong></a>. 
					The <a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSolarWindhtm')"><strong>solar wind</strong></a> 
					can effect the magnetic field of  Earth.
				</p>
			</span>
