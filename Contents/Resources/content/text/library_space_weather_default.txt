<div id="research">
		
	<div id="term">
		<h2>Space Weather</h2>
	</div>
	
	<div id="term-content">
		
		<div id="full-pane">
		
			<span class="caption right">
				<img src="../images/library_space_weather.jpg" alt="Spaceweather" />
				<small class="center">Aurora as seen from space.</small>
			</span>
			<span id="definition">
				<p>
					<strong>Definition:</strong><br />
					A collection of processes and events often driven by solar activity, which can affect space and the near-Earth environment. 
					Some of the possible effects of space weather are damage to satellites and disruption of power grids on Earth.
				</p>
			</span>
			<span id="more-information">
				<p>
					<strong>More Information:</strong><br />
					<strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSolarFlareshtm')">Solar flares</a></strong> and 
					<strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchCoronalMassEjectionshtm')">coronal mass ejections (CMEs)</a></strong> 
					can create space weather when aimed at Earth. 
				</p>
				<p>
					We need to monitor space weather because it can seriously damage spacecraft components and <strong>
					<a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSatellitehtm')">satellites</a></strong> in Earth orbit.
				</p>
				<p>
					Charged particles from the <strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchSunhtm')">Sun</a></strong> 
					interact with Earth's <strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchMagneticFieldhtm')">magnetic field </a></strong>
					generating <strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchGeomagneticStormhtm')">geomagnetic storms</a></strong> 
					that cause intense and beautiful <strong><a href="event:loadToolCItem('Research','ResearchAurorashtm')">aurora</a></strong>.
				</p>
				<p>
					Geomagnetic storms can damage sensitive electronics on satellites and spacecraft.  
					They can create currents in the ground, affecting electrical power grids worldwide. 
				</p>
			</span>
			
		</div>
